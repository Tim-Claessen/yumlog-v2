A dump-and-go curry that earns its depth from one step you shouldn't skip: bloom the onion and spices in a pan first. Five minutes of effort, and it's the difference between flat and proper. Chickpeas and sweet potato hold up to the long cook. Spinach goes in at the end so it stays green.

## Ingredients

- 1 tbsp coconut or vegetable oil
- 1 large brown onion, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tbsp curry powder (or 1 heaped tbsp garam masala + 1 tsp ground cumin + 1 tsp ground coriander)
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/2 tsp chilli flakes, to taste
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 x 400g tins chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 large sweet potato (about 500g), peeled and cut into 3cm chunks
- 1 x 400g tin crushed tomatoes
- 1 x 400ml tin full-fat coconut milk
- 1 tsp sea salt, plus more to taste
- 150g baby spinach
- Juice of 1/2 lime
- Coriander, to finish

## Instructions

1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium. Cook the onion for 5 minutes until soft and translucent.
2. Add garlic, ginger, curry powder, turmeric, chilli, and tomato paste. Stir for 1 to 2 minutes until fragrant. This is the step that matters. Don't skip it.
3. Tip the lot into the slow cooker. Add chickpeas, sweet potato, crushed tomatoes, coconut milk, and salt. Stir to combine.
4. Cook on LOW for 6 hours, or HIGH for 3 to 4 hours, until the sweet potato is tender.
5. Stir through the spinach in the last 10 minutes until wilted.
6. Finish with lime juice. Taste, adjust salt. Top with coriander.
7. Serve with rice or naan.

## Tips

- Bloom the spices in step 2. Long, wet slow cooking won't develop that base flavour on its own.
- Use full-fat coconut milk. Light splits and tastes thin over a long cook.
- Slow cookers trap steam, so this can run watery. If it does, lift the lid for the last 30 minutes on HIGH, or mash a few chickpeas and sweet potato chunks against the side to thicken.
- Frozen spinach works. Squeeze it dry first or it waters down the sauce.
